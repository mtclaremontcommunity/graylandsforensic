One claim in the reply, checked against its own source

The reply describes Infrastructure WA's assessment of the business case as follows. We looked up the assessment itself.

Minister Carey's office, 24 July 2026
…recognised the strong strategic merit of the proposal and the need for additional forensic mental health capacity.
That sentence is drawn from the paragraph below. Nothing else from this paragraph appears in the reply.
Infrastructure WA, MIPA Summary Assessment Report — same paragraph, March 2023
The proposal has strong strategic merit…reflecting the urgent need for additional forensic mental health beds. However, as a result of the rapid pace of business case development…further development [of scope, cost, timing, procurement strategy, expected benefits and program management] is required. As a consequence, IWA considers that the business case on its own contains insufficient information on which to base an investment decision.
Source: Infrastructure WA, MIPA Summary Assessment Report, March 2023 (infrastructure.wa.gov.au) — read the full six-page report at the link.

City of Nedlands

We also wrote to the City of Nedlands on 17 June 2026, asking ten questions about what the Council had been told about the project and what role it had played. The Mayor and the Director of City Development and Regulatory Services replied on 17 July 2026, answering all ten directly.

What information was the Council given, and by whom?
The City has received limited information — primarily correspondence, stakeholder notifications, and material already publicly released by State Government agencies. It has not been given the full business case, the masterplan (beyond what was attached to one notification), or the detailed project assessments the community has asked about. Information was directed to the CEO and relevant officers, and received progressively over time rather than as a single formal briefing.
Were elected members briefed, or was this considered at a Council meeting?
No. The newly elected Council has not been briefed, and the matter has not been considered at any council briefing, committee meeting, or public council meeting.
Has the Council taken a formal position, or been asked to consult residents and schools on the State's behalf?
No formal Council position has been adopted. The Council has not been requested by the State Government to consult residents, schools, or parents-and-citizens associations on the project's behalf, and is not aware of any advice suggesting that its lack of comment would be treated as support for, or consultation on, the proposal.
What planning material has the Council actually received?
No development application, public works referral, WAPC referral, heritage material, traffic assessment, or environmental material — only the Infrastructure WA summary assessment report, attached to a notification.

Unlike the reply above, every question we asked the Council was answered directly. Where the answer was "no" or "not yet," it was given as a plain no — not left open.
