Take action

Two things. Together, they carry real weight.

Sign the petition, then register your support below. They do different jobs. The petition shows scale — a public number anyone can point to, including sceptics who'll ask how many are bots or non-residents. Registering support does what a petition count can't: it puts your name and suburb on record as an actual Mount Claremont resident who took this seriously — the kind of evidence a government finds much harder to wave away.

Step 1 — 30 seconds

Sign the petition

The petition is hosted on Change.org and sets out the same asks documented in the Research Paper: pause the works, release the evidence, and consult the community properly before Stage 2 proceeds.

Sign the petition ↗
Step 2 — one minute

Register your support

This is not just a newsletter sign-up. When we write to Ministers, MPs, or media, "a petition with X thousand signatures" is easy to dismiss — anyone, anywhere, can click a button. Being able to say "residents across Mount Claremont, Karrakatta and Swanbourne have formally registered their concern, by name" is not. That's the difference this makes. You'll also receive factual updates as the formal processes — Questions on Notice, FOI requests, the Heritage and Development Application windows — play out.

Form not loading? Open it in a new tab, or email mtclaremontcommunitynetwork@gmail.com and we'll add you directly.

What this information is used for, and what it isn't.

  • Your name and suburb may be cited — individually or grouped by area ("residents of X and Y streets") — in our correspondence with Ministers, MPs, and media. This is the actual point of registering: real, identifiable local support is what a signature count alone can't demonstrate.
  • We will not publish your name publicly, or hand your individual details to anyone outside the small group of volunteers running this campaign, without asking you first.
  • Your details are never sold and never used for anything beyond this campaign.
  • Remove yourself at any time — email mtclaremontcommunitynetwork@gmail.com and you'll be taken off the list and out of anything we're preparing.
  • This is run by a volunteer resident group, not a registered organisation — we're handling your information with the same care a formal one would, and wanted you to know exactly who's holding it.

Other ways to help

  • Write to Sandra Brewer MLA (Member for Cottesloe) and your federal Member for Curtin
  • Ask your school's P&C or Board to write formally to the responsible Ministers
  • Write to the Heritage Council of Western Australia — this process is still open, unlike the closed EPBC referral
  • Share this website with neighbours and other school communities — the research paper and evidence pack are both downloadable from here
  • Check the latest updates to see what's already been sent and what replies have come back, before you write in yourself

We support forensic mental health services being properly resourced in Western Australia. Our concern is whether this site, this process, and this evidence base meet the standard the public is entitled to before an irreversible $698 million decision proceeds — not politics, and not the patients.
