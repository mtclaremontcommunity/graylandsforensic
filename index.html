$698 million.  Four schools.  Zero consultation.

A $698 million decision the community was never shown.

The State Government is progressing a major forensic mental health expansion at Graylands, in the middle of a residential suburb. Stage 1 is already in design. Before construction proceeds, residents and school communities deserve the full evidence, the safety plan, and genuine consultation.

Sign the petition to ask the Government to pause the works, release the evidence, and consult the community properly.

Where we stand

We support forensic mental health services being properly resourced in Western Australia. Our question is whether this site, this process, and this evidence base are adequate — not whether the patients deserve care.

In 60 seconds
  • Graylands is proposed to become WA's only large-scale forensic mental health campus.
  • Stage 1 would take forensic beds from about 45 to about 85.
  • The long-term plan is 136 forensic beds and 40 mental health recovery beds, at an estimated total capital cost of $698.3 million.
  • Four schools — one just 270 metres away — sit within 1km of the campus.
  • No formal public consultation with residents or schools has been identified in the public record.
  • The community is asking the Government to pause, release the evidence, and consult properly before the design is locked in.

What's actually being planned

Graylands currently holds 45 forensic patients across the Frankland Centre and Dryandra Ward. A funded Stage 1 Infrastructure WA, Mar 2023 adds 40 new beds, taking that to approximately 85. Infrastructure WA's own assessment confirms the government's full long-term plan for the site: 136 forensic beds and 40 mental health recovery beds, closing 118 existing general psychiatric beds, at an estimated total cost of $698.3 million Infrastructure WA, Mar 2023. Only Stage 1 is funded. The remaining beds — Stage 2 — are not.

No formal public consultation with residents or schools has been identified in the public record at any stage of this five-year process MHC Taskforce record.

Eight documented concerns

Each one is drawn from a government document, a parliamentary record, or credible reporting — not from speculation. Click through for the short version, or download the full paper for the complete sourced analysis.

CONCERN 01

The law changed after the plan was made

The CLMI Act 2023 received Royal Assent the same month as the funding commitment.

 CONCERN 02

The government's own advisor found the case insufficient

Infrastructure WA flagged six gaps. Funding followed three weeks later.

 CONCERN 03

A promised decommissioning was reversed, unexplained

The site was funded for closure studies in 2019, then expanded instead.

 CONCERN 04

The community was never consulted

Five years of planning; no public meeting held in Mount Claremont.

 CONCERN 05

Environmental and heritage concerns

Threatened black cockatoo habitat cleared; heritage costs unresolved.

 CONCERN 06

Community safety protocols are undisclosed

A 2014 escape went unreported to the community for a week.

 CONCERN 07

No independent property impact assessment

IWA found the social impact assessment incomplete.

 CONCERN 08

The site was never assessed for security

Twenty sites were compared on transport access — not safety.
